Here's a timeline of MCA's life:

  • 1964 - Born in Brooklyn, New York City. His father was an architect, and his mother was a social worker.
  • 1978 - Started Edward R. Murrow High School in Brooklyn's Midwood neighborhood. There he taught himself to play the bass guitar.
  • 1981 - Formed the Beastie Boys with John Berry, Kate Schellenbach, and Michael Diamond. They played their first showâ€”while still a hardcore punk band.
  • 1983 - Attended Bard College for two years before dropping out.
  • 1986 - The Beastie Boys, released their first album, Licensed to Ill, on Def Jam Records.
  • 1995 - While attending a speech by the Dalai Lama at Harvard University, he met his wife, Tibetan American Dechen Wangdu.
  • 1998 - Married and had their daughter, Tenzin Losel.
  • 1998 - When receiving the Video Vanguard Award at the MTV Music Awards, the practicing Buddhist condemned America's wars in Muslim countries and prejudice against Muslims and Arabs.
  • 2002 - Constructed a recording studio in New York City called Oscilloscope Laboratories. He began an independent film distributing company called Oscilloscope Pictures.
  • 2006 - Directed the Beastie Boys concert film, Awesome; I Fuckin' Shot That!
  • 2008 - Directed the 2008 film Gunnin' For That #1 Spot about eight high school basketball prospects at the Boost Mobile Elite 24 Hoops Classic in Harlem, New York City.
  • 2009 - 2009, Yauch was diagnosed with a cancerous parotid gland and lymph node.
  • 2010 - The Beastie Boys had sold 40 million records worldwide.
  • 2011 - Received the Charles Flint Kellogg Award in Arts and Letters from Bard College, given "in recognition of a significant contribution to the American artistic or literary heritage".
  • 2012 - The group was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. Yauch was inducted in absentia due to his illness. His bandmates paid tribute to him.
  • 2012 - Died at age 47 on May 4. Upon his death, fellow musicians and artists paid tribute.
